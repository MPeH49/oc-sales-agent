The Journey

Four years. Nine estates. One founding thesis.

2021
Where it All Started

Founded on 2 June 2021. The Green Print — a 30,000-word founding document — laid out how to build a commercial enterprise delivering positive environmental and social impact first, with profit as a result, not the purpose. Five estates acquired: Leighon, Wood Advent, Swineley, Esgair Arth, Firth of Tay.

2022
From Concept to Reality

Two landmark Scottish acquisitions — Invergeldie (11,701 acres) and Blackburn & Hartsgarth (11,407 acres) — changed the scale of everything. Award-winning debt funding package from Triodos Bank proved this model was bankable.

2023
Balancing the Portfolio

Added Mornacott (Devon) and Shropham (Norfolk) — property, ecotourism, and BNG potential. The Oxygen Escapes ecotourism brand began to take shape. Every tree GPS-mapped; the evidentiary standard that would later command record prices.

2024
Testing the Model

Siblyback (Cornwall) and Project Skyfall (15,035 acres, Cairngorms) pushed the portfolio to 45,000 acres. 220 acres of new native woodland. 23,000 native trees planted at Leighon alone.

2025
Finding the Voice

A transaction achieved a world-record £125/tonne price for Woodland Carbon Credits — because we could prove every single step in the chain. Portfolio approaching £400m. Foundations laid for Oxygen Capital (institutional capital) and Oxygen Intelligence (data platform).

Recent Critiques

Pricing
March 2026

"Your £125/t price is not what most UK credits trade at. This is cherry-picking."

Submitted by an anonymous institutional buyer. The Beyond Carbon transaction was a negotiated deal with unusual co-benefits. Not representative of market floor.

Our response: Fair. We've updated pricing language across the site to distinguish "proof-point transactions" from "market average", and added the Carbon Curve as the benchmark for forward pricing rather than quoting individual deals as generalisable.

Methodology
February 2026

"Individual-tree LiDAR is expensive overkill. Customers don't care about cm precision."

Submitted by Dr. M. Rees, independent carbon methodologist. Argues consumer-facing claim complexity exceeds regulatory need.

Our response: Disagree — but sharpened the language. The LiDAR precision is load-bearing for institutional buyers and the evidentiary spine of the whole operation. For consumer UX, we've added a plain-English explanation layer without removing the underlying detail.

Claims
January 2026

"'The highest quality carbon credits in the world' is an unsupportable claim."

Submitted by a climate NGO. Argues the claim is marketing hyperbole and requires a specific source or third-party verification.

Our response: Accepted. The claim is now sourced to independent rating (BeZero / Sylvera public ratings of UK nature-based credits from OC's portfolio, 2025). Where the ratings change, the claim will change.

Critiques published here are edited only for length and clarity. Nothing is filtered for tone.
